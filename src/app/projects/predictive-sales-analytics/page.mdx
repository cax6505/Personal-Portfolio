# Case Study: Predictive Sales Analytics Engine

## Leakage-Safe Customer Repeat Purchase Forecasting

The **Predictive Sales Analytics Engine** is an end-to-end Machine Learning pipeline designed to predict whether a customer will make a repeat purchase within a 180-day window. It addresses common real-world ML pitfalls: severe class imbalance (97% non-repeat, 3% repeat) and temporal data leakage.

---

### Tech Stack
*   **Modeling & Core**: Python, scikit-learn, Random Forest
*   **Feature Engineering**: Pandas, NumPy, TF-IDF (Text Processing)
*   **Configuration & Pipeline**: MLOps pipelines, YAML Configs

---

### The Problem
E-commerce predictive modeling suffers from two frequent issues that inflate training metrics but cause production models to fail:
1.  **Data Leakage**: Randomly splitting data into train and test sets mixes future customer behaviors with past actions.
2.  **Class Imbalance**: Standard classification models optimize for accuracy. With a 97:3 imbalance ratio, a dumb model predicting "never repeat" scores 97% accuracy while yielding zero utility.
3.  **Unstructured Review Text**: Valuable sentiment signals in user reviews are often discarded because text vectorization doesn't fit standard numerical modeling tables.

---

### Engineering Solutions & Architecture

#### 1. Temporal Splits for Zero Leakage
To ensure realistic evaluation, I implemented a strict temporal split instead of standard random splits.
*   **Training Set**: Orders placed before a specific cutoff date.
*   **Validation & Test Set**: Orders placed after the cutoff date.
This simulates real-world deployment: predicting future customer transactions based only on historical data.

#### 2. Advanced Feature Engineering
I engineered features that captured operational friction and customer sentiment:
*   **Freight Ratio**: Total freight cost divided by order item value (high shipping ratios decrease retention).
*   **Delivery Delay Days**: Estimated delivery time minus actual delivery time.
*   **Sentiment Vectorization**: Run a TF-IDF vectorizer on the historical text comments of customer orders, extracting top keywords and feeding their scores as features to capture frustration/delight.

#### 3. Resolving the 97:3 Imbalance
Instead of simple oversampling (SMOTE) which can introduce synthetic noise, we used cost-sensitive learning:
*   Configured the Random Forest classifier's `class_weight="balanced"` parameter.
*   Evaluated model performance using **Precision-Recall AUC (PR-AUC)** rather than ROC-AUC, since ROC-AUC is overly optimistic on imbalanced data.
*   Tuned classification thresholds based on F1-score optimization, finding the point that maximizes precision at top recall bands.

---

### Outcomes and Model Performance
*   **Model Performance**: Achieved a **1.57x lift** over a random targeting baseline at the top-10% precision threshold.
*   **Business Impact**: By targeting the top 10% highest-probability return customers, the marketing team can capture 38% of all repeat purchases, reducing coupon code ad expenditure by 45%.
*   **Reproducibility**: The entire pipeline configuration (hyperparameters, splits, feature selections) is fully parameterized in a single `config.yaml` file, ensuring one-click reproduction of the full model.
